Hi, I’m Shaman.

Environmental science, forests, geospatial data, and teaching.

I’m a graduate student in Environmental Science at the University of Michigan, building on a B.S. in Environmental Science with a minor in Computer Science. My work sits at the intersection of ecology, geospatial data, and decision-making: from modeling tree growth and climate to mapping biomass and designing forest management plans.

Outside the classroom, I’ve taught restoration ecology and woody plants labs, worked with students in FIRST Robotics, and led environmental education programs at camps and nature centers. A lot of my free time is spent rock climbing, biking, exploring Michigan forests, or tinkering on projects in the woodshop.
